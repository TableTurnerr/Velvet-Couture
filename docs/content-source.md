## Our Story

### The 100-Year Story of Dallas's BEST Iraqi Bakery and Breakfast Cafe

*We make phenomenal breads*

### It All Started in Baghdad, 1919

Our story begins not in Texas, but in Baghdad — in 1919 — where our family first opened a bakery. *Albaghdady* — البغدادي — simply means *the one from Baghdad*, and for over a century, that name has meant the same thing wherever we've stood: hand-made breads, Iraqi sweets, and delicious Iraqi dishes.

### Authentic Traditional Iraqi Sweets

The recipes have passed from one generation to the next — same techniques, same standards, same insistence that bread is sacred. The breads, the baklava, the ladyfingers stuffed with cream — these aren't recipes we found. They've been in our family since 1919, kept alive through war, migration, and the patience of teaching the next pair of hands.

### Representing Iraqi Heritage and Family Recipes in Texas

Our father and master baker Salah Hassan, grew up learning about our traditional recipes and has been perfecting his craft for 50 years, following in the footsteps of his father. As D Magazine noted, he 'quietly turns out his wares' with a skill that only comes from a lifetime of tradition. When you visit, you aren't just getting bread; you're getting a century of expertise from a true professional.

In 2012, he brought the family trade to Texas, opening a small storefront on Greenville Avenue in Richardson — two parking spots and a counter — and began to bake the way our family has always baked.

### ضيافة — The Reason We Bake

For our father, this was never just about selling bread. It was about *ḍiyāfa* — Iraqi hospitality. The belief that no one should leave your table hungry, that bread is sacred, that guests are a gift. He wanted his customers in Texas to taste what he tasted growing up in Baghdad — the warmth of a kitchen where everything is made by hand.

### Taste of Iraq in the Heart of Dallas, Texas

For over fifteen years now, our small breakfast place beside the barber shop has been a quiet landmark — a place loyal customers drive across Greenville Ave for, and where new neighbors discover their first bite of real Iraqi baking.

### Come See Us in the Morning, When the Bread is Hottest

The best time to find us is early, when the bread is freshest and the trays are just out. Come in for breakfast — a warm *samoon*, a piece of *baklava*, a *ladyfinger* with your chai — and let the day start the way it should and end your day with delicious kunafa in the evening.

## Menu Item Descriptions (for order + menu page)

**Baklava** — Our flagship. Dozens of layers of buttery, hand-rolled phyllo, fresh pistachios and walnuts, finished with our family's signature honey syrup. The recipe we've been making since 1919.

**Pistachio Baklava** — Our most-ordered variety. Generous layers of fresh-ground pistachios between paper-thin phyllo, soaked in just enough syrup. The bright green crown on every Iraqi dessert tray.

**Bread Baklava** — A Richardson favorite and a signature Iraqi twist on traditional baklava. A golden bread base, soaked in honey syrup, layered with sweet cream and chopped pistachios. Rich, comforting, and one of the most-requested desserts in our case.

**Ladyfingers (Znood El Sit)** — Delicate phyllo rolls wrapped around pistachios, baked deep gold, then dipped in syrup. Lighter than baklava, twice as elegant.

**Kunafa** — Shredded phyllo, melted cheese, and warm syrup, baked golden and served hot. We make it to order so it arrives the way it's meant to — stretchy, fragrant, and impossible to share politely.

**Burma** — Long, cigar-rolled phyllo packed with pistachios and finished with syrup. Crisp, nutty, and one of the lesser-known Iraqi sweets that earns instant fans.

**Mabrouma** — A coiled phyllo pastry layered with pistachios, baked until deep gold. A traditional Iraqi dessert most often spotted on celebration trays.

**Fatayer** — Hand-folded savory pies baked fresh daily. Choose from spinach (sabanekh), cheese, or seasoned ground meat. Soft, golden, and perfect with chai.

**Manakish** — Flatbread topped with za'atar, cheese, or meat — Middle Eastern breakfast at its most authentic. A weekend staple in our Richardson bakery.

**Samoon (Iraqi Bread)** — Diamond-shaped tandoor bread with a crisp crust and a soft, chewy inside. Baked fresh every morning. The bread we grew up on.

## Breakfast Menu Descriptions

**Omlet (مخلمة)** — An Iraqi-style omelette done right. Ground meat, fresh tomatoes, onions, and eggs cooked together until tender and rich. Heartier than your usual omelette, and the perfect way to start a slow morning.

**Chelfry (جلفراي)** — A traditional Iraqi morning hash of slow-cooked lamb pieces, golden potatoes, onions, and tomatoes. Bold, savory, and the kind of plate that warms you from the inside out.

**Baqila (باقلاء بالدهن)** — Iraq's most-loved breakfast. Tender fava beans simmered in their own broth, served with eggs, crispy fried onions, and warm bread soaked in the broth. A national tradition, served the way it's meant to be.

**Bastirma (باسترما بالبيض)** — Spiced cured beef sausage, sizzled with eggs until the edges crisp. A Middle Eastern breakfast classic with deep, smoky flavor in every bite.

**Potato with Egg (بطاطا مع بيض)** — Simple, comforting, and exactly what you want on a busy morning. Pan-fried potatoes scrambled with farm-fresh eggs. Pairs perfectly with hot chai.

**Tomato with Egg (طماطم مع بيض)** — Ripe tomatoes slow-cooked with eggs into a rich, savory scramble. Light, fresh, and a Middle Eastern breakfast staple for good reason.

**Fried Kabab (كباب عروق)** — Hand-formed patties of seasoned ground meat with fresh parsley and onions, pan-fried golden and crisp at the edges. A traditional Iraqi favorite, all day flavor in a breakfast portion.

**Albaghdady Plate (طبق البغدادي)** — Our signature breakfast spread and the plate we're named for. Tender ground meat kabab, golden potatoes, eggplant tomato stew, and two pieces of our handmade kubbah — all on one tray. A full Iraqi feast before noon.

**Kahi & Qeimar (كاهي وقيمر)** — Iraq's most beloved sweet breakfast. Layers of flaky golden kahi pastry drizzled with syrup, served with rich clotted cream (qeimar). A morning indulgence our family has been making since 1919.

**Qeimar & Debes (قيمر ودبس)** — A traditional Iraqi sweet breakfast — fresh clotted cream and pure date syrup, served with a piece of warm samoon to scoop it all up. Simple, ancient, unforgettable.

**Kubba (كبة)** — Four hand-rolled pieces of our famous Iraqi kubba — bulgur shells stuffed with seasoned meat, onions, and herbs, fried until golden and crisp. The dish every Iraqi grandmother has an opinion about.

**Beef & Egg (لحم وبيض)** — Tender beef shawarma topped with a perfectly cooked egg. A modern Iraqi breakfast classic — savory, satisfying, and ready to fuel your whole morning.

**Lamb Liver (معلاك غنم)** — A traditional Middle Eastern breakfast — fresh lamb liver sautéed with onions and tomatoes until tender and flavorful. Bold, hearty, and the way Baghdadi mornings have started for generations.
